Grammar

Particle / Ending Rule Example

은 (eun) / 는 (neun)

Used as topic marker.


– 은 (eun) : after consonant
– 는 (neun): after vowel
나 (na) → 나는 (naneun)
책 (chaek) → 책은 (chaekeun)

Sample Sentences for 는 (neun):


나 [는] 학생이에요
(na[neun] haksaengieyo)
I am a student.

Sample Sentences for 은 (eun):


우리 선생님 [은] 미국인이에요
(uri seonsaengnim[eun] migukinieyo)
Our teacher is American.

이 (i) / 가 (ga)

Used as subject marker


– 이 (i) : after consonant
– 가 (ga): after vowel
사람 (saram) → 사람이 (sarami)
버스 (beoseu) → 버스가 (beoseuga)

Sample Sentences for 이 (i):


가방 [이] 무거워요
(gabang[i] mugeowoyo)
The beg is heavy.

Sample Sentences for 가 (ga):


누 [가] 아파요?
(nu[ga] apayo)
Who is sick?

이에요 (ieyo) / 예요 (yeyo)

Polite ending meaning 'to be'.


– 이에요 (ieyo): after consonant
– 예요 (yeyo): after vowel
학생 (haksaeng) → 학생이에요 (haksaeng-ieyo)
의사 (uisa) → 의사예요 (uisa-yeyo)

Sample Sentences for 이에요 (ieyo):


저는 학생 [이에요]
Jeoneun haksaeng [ieyo]
I am a student

Sample Sentences for 에요 (yeyo):


제 취미는 독서 [예요]
Je chwimineun dokseo [yeyo]
My hobby is reading

Dialogue

Jane has just moved into a new home, and as she steps outside to get some fresh air, he meets her neighbour, Nana for the first time. The two of them started chatting casually:


Jane

안녕하세요. 저는 제인이에요. 새 이웃이에요.
Hello. I am Jane, your new neighbour.
안녕하세요. 저는 나나예요.
Hello. I am Nana.

Nana

Jane

만나서 반가워요, 나나 씨.
Nice to meet you, Nana.
만나서 반가워요.
Nice to meet you.

Nana

Jane

어느 나라 사람이에요?
Which country are you from?
저는 한국 사람이에요.
I am Korean.

Nana

Jane

몇 살이에요?
How old are you?
저는 스물여섯 살이에요.
I am 26 years old.

Nana

Jane

직업이 뭐예요?
What is your job?
저는 선생님이에요.
I am a teacher.

Nana

Jane

안녕히 계세요. 나중에 봐요.
Bye. See you later.
안녕히 가세요.
Bye.

Nana
